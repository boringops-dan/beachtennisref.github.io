# How big is a beach tennis court?

**Short Answer:** A beach tennis court is 16 meters long and 8 meters wide, the same dimensions for both singles and doubles. The net height is 1.7 meters at the posts and 1.65 meters at the center.

## Full Explanation

Per ITF Beach Tennis Court Specifications (Appendix I of the ITF Beach Tennis Rules), the court is 16 meters long (8 meters on each side of the net) and 8 meters wide. Unlike lawn tennis, beach tennis uses identical court dimensions for both singles and doubles -- there are no separate singles sidelines. The net is 1.70 meters high at the posts and 1.65 meters high at the center. The court surface is sand. Lines on the court are typically tape or painted rope. All boundary lines are considered good (a ball touching any line is in).

**Ruleset:** ITF
**Rule Reference:** ITF Beach Tennis Rules, Appendix I (Court Specifications)
