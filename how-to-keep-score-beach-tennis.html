The Basics

Scoring a Match, Step by Step.

  1. Decide the first server and ends. A quick spin or toss. one team serves first, the other picks an end.
  2. Score each point 15, 30, 40, game. remembering it is no-ad, so 40-40 is decided by the next point.
  3. Pass serve each game, alternating the server within a doubles pair across your team's service games.
  4. Change ends after odd total games (1, 3, 5, ...) so sun and wind stay fair.
  5. In the tie-break, follow Rule 8b. serve alternates every two points after the first. This is where most hand-scored games go wrong.

The Problem With Doing It In Your Head

Between rallies, in the sun, with friends chatting. it is genuinely hard to hold serve order, the score, and the next ends change at once. Paper helps a little; a generic tennis app gets the beach tennis nuances (no-ad, the match tie-break, Rule 8b) wrong.
